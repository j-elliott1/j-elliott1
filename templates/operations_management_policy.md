# Operations Management Policy

This policy establishes the framework for consistent, secure, and efficient operations management practices across the organization.  
It ensures that all operational activities align with business objectives, risk tolerance, and compliance requirements.

---

## Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to define the principles, standards, and responsibilities for managing operational activities.  
It promotes efficiency, accountability, and continuous improvement within all business and technical operations.

This policy supports:
- Clear operational governance and accountability  
- Proactive risk identification and mitigation  
- Compliance with internal and external standards  
- Continuous service and process improvement  

---

## Scope

This policy applies to all personnel, departments, systems, and third-party vendors involved in the organization’s operational processes, including but not limited to:

- IT Asset Management (ITAM) and Software Asset Management (SAM)  
- Vendor and Contract Management  
- Data and Configuration Management  
- Service Delivery and Performance Monitoring  
- Change, Incident, and Problem Management  

---

## Guiding Principles

1. **Operational Excellence** – All operations are designed for efficiency, repeatability, and measurable improvement.  
2. **Risk Awareness** – Operational planning and execution must consider potential risks and apply mitigation strategies.  
3. **Transparency** – Processes and outcomes must be documented, traceable, and reviewable.  
4. **Compliance and Governance** – All operations must adhere to internal policies, applicable laws, and regulatory standards.  
5. **Collaboration and Accountability** – Clear ownership and cross-functional communication ensure aligned decision-making.  

---

## Roles and Responsibilities

| **Role** | **Responsibility** |
|-----------|--------------------|
| **Operations Manager / Director** | Oversees operational governance, policy adherence, and performance metrics. |
| **Process Owners** | Maintain Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) and ensure compliance within their domains. |
| **Project / Program Managers** | Ensure project-level processes align with operational standards and risk policies. |
| **Risk & Compliance Officer** | Monitors adherence to risk management and compliance frameworks. |
| **All Personnel** | Follow established procedures, report incidents, and contribute to continuous improvement. |

---

## Policy Statements

1. **Standardization:** All core operations must be documented through approved SOPs or playbooks.  
2. **Change Management:** No operational change shall be implemented without an approved Change Request and risk review.  
3. **Risk Tracking:** All identified risks must be recorded in the [Risk Register](../templates/SAM_RISK_REGISTER.md) and reviewed monthly.  
4. **Vendor Oversight:** All third-party vendors must complete a [Vendor Risk Questionnaire](../templates/VENDOR_RISK_QUESTIONNAIRE.md) before contract approval.  
5. **Incident Management:** Incidents must be documented within 24 hours, with lessons learned captured in the Operations Log.  
6. **Continuous Improvement:** Teams must review performance metrics quarterly and implement at least one process improvement per cycle.  
7. **Data Integrity:** All operational data must be validated against authoritative sources (e.g., SCCM, ServiceNow, AD).  
8. **Audit Readiness:** Operations must remain audit-ready, maintaining verifiable documentation for all activities.  

---

## Compliance and Performance Monitoring

- **Metrics:** Operations performance will be measured using KPIs such as SLA adherence, incident closure time, and audit compliance rate.  
- **Audits:** Internal audits will be conducted semi-annually or following any major operational event.  
- **Non-Compliance:** Deviations from this policy must be documented, reviewed, and remediated within 30 days.
